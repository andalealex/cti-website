Intelligence Buy-in

Modeling threat scenarios develops a picture on how critical assets can become compromised based on the current threat landscape. These findings still need to be presented and approved by executives. This summary will allow executives to understand the high level components of an intelligence-based security strategy and provide details about the following questions:

  • What is the current threat landscape?
  • How often do organizations suffer security breaches?
  • How is this changing the strategies that organizations must take to move into intelligence?
  • What are some real data breach costs for organizations?
  • How can intelligence based security reduce the average cost of a data breach for your organization?

Below we have attempted to answer these questions in regards to Banner Health:

  • Banner Health has previously been hacked in 2014 where 55,207 people were affected
    • SSN’s and Medicare numbers printed on magazine
  • As of July 2016, 3.7 million people’s information has been stolen due to the Banner Health Hack which resulted in the loss of credit card information
    • resulting in lawsuits from physicians and patients
    • offering a year free of credit and fraud monitoring services
  • That’s a 67% increase in cyber attacks in 2 years!
  • Banner Health will lose 3.97 million in loss of patient information and another 5.55 million in settlements
  • In order to prevent such attacks like these in the future, Banner Health needs a strategy to combat the rise in breaches and overall cybersecurity inefficiencies through the following roadmap:
    • Predict: determine the attack before it happens, understand the hackers: honeypots, understand threats
    • Prevent: prevent an attack from happening with antivirus, patches, update software/tools, vulnerability assessments
    • Detect: identify attacks that were not prevented through IDS, analyzing log files
    • Respond: Immediately address incidents to minimize losses
