Key Insights & Intelligence

The identified analytical approaches selected for the aerospace and defense industry are: Malware Analysis, Event Correlation, and Anomaly Detection. These approaches were selected because they will help organizations within this industry make timely, relevant, and effective decisions considering their cybersecurity posture.

Based on our analysis, the following is a summary of our updated key insights and intelligence as it relates to our CTI project:

Emerging Industry Threats: Increased Social Engineering & Web Compromises

Through our analysis, we have identified an increase in social engineering attacks via spear phishing emails and web compromises with the intentions for the adversary (Chinese hacker groups) to obtain classified material, data, and intellectual property. About 45% of the APTs analyzed also indicated that the adversaries attempted strategic web compromises, zero day attacks, and tried taking advantage of newly exposed exploits. Therefore, we must be aware of the hackers combining strategies and utilizing a combination of tools (like SOGU, Gh0st, PHOTO, etc) to achieve their attacks.

Anomalies in Employee Logins

Analyzing user logins and log files throughout the span of a typical work day shows that there were some anomalies that appeared early in the day and later in the evening. These instances allow for the identification of and can start the investigation of potential malicious behavior of an adversary, such as a hacker group or insider threats, who are seeking to gain access to or steal IP.

Emerging Industry Threats: Indicators of Compromise

Through event correlation, we analyzed attacks occurring against the Pulse Secure VPN service, which is widely used by the aerospace & defense industry. The primary adversary in this scenario is state sponsored threat actor UNC2630 whose primary goals are to maintain long-term access to networks, collect credentials and steal proprietary data. About 93% of the indicators of compromise were related to File Hashes which indicate that we must focus on these types of IoCs moving forward.
