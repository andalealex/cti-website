WiGLE

WiGLE contains Open Source information relating to WiFi access points collected by a community of interest globally. The people who collect this data are called wardrivers. 802.11 WiFi and cellular infrastructure are the target of collection, and the goal is to educate the general public on why WiFi and other cybersecurity practices are vital.

Two different approaches were used by the team members for WiGLE. They are both repeatable and sustainable with the help of other OSINT tools.

  • Using known locations of corporate or industrial headquarters and other facilities to search for 802.11 WiFi access points associated to the firms of interest.
  • Using data gathered from Shodan along with popular naming conventions and boolean logic to find WiFi access points associated with other IoT devices.

WiFi security protocols may be a good indicator to security posture and provide a possible attack vector for APTs or other malign actors to exploit with on the ground capability and connectivity to that access point.
